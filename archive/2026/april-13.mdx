## Counter-intuitive findings

**All 8 AI benchmarks can be gamed for near-perfect scores.** Berkeley's team systematically audited SWE-bench, WebArena, OSWorld, GAIA, and four others. Every single one can be exploited without solving any actual tasks. This means every model comparison, investment decision, and technology selection based on benchmark scores over the past year may have been built on sand. For indie developers, the implication is clear: don't chase leaderboards; test models against your own real-world scenarios.

**A single .md file earned 7,319 GitHub stars.** [andrej-karpathy-skills](https://github.com/forrestchang/andrej-karpathy-skills) contains just one CLAUDE.md file. More stars than many fully-developed products that took months to build. Content is not code, but content can be a product.

**VCs reject you because you're too profitable.** @tradertef opens his 842-point article with: "I was rejected from yet another pitch night. The feedback was simply: 'What do you even need funding for?'" The extreme lean bootstrapper enters a paradox — margins too high to attract investment, because VCs invest in growth potential, not current profit. 468 comments show this resonates deeply.

**macOS tool users' aversion to subscriptions is structural, not a price issue.** The entire boringBar comment section (165 comments) almost exclusively discussed the pricing model. The product was well-received; the subscription scared everyone away. @fii: "For free, I can use Alfred/Raycast, Aerospace, and sketchybar and have all this functionality." The developer switched to perpetual licensing the same day. This isn't user feedback — it's a market verdict.
